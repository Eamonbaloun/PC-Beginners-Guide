### Parts Lists

üö® **Top Recommendations for Builds** üö®

I wanted to make a quick guide on what I think makes a solid build. The key to a good build is smart spending. This isn‚Äôt about specific parts, but more of a general outline of what you should aim for. *[More of anything is always better‚Äîit keeps you from buying twice!]* Make sure to put together a part list on [PC Part Picker](https://pcpartpicker.com) and share it on forums. People will help you fine-tune your build‚ÄîI help people with their part lists all the time on Reddit. Just be clear about what you want to do with your computer and the games or programs you plan to use.

- **CPU**: Go for at least 8 cores, but I recommend 12 if you can. It‚Äôll give you more headroom for multitasking and gaming.

- **GPU**: The big question: [Nvidia or AMD](https://youtu.be/BJ75QVCip88)? Personally, I‚Äôve never bought an AMD GPU because I find Nvidia‚Äôs price premium worth it for better drivers, stability, and features (DLSS). That said, I respect what AMD is doing with better specs at lower prices. For a recommendation, 12GB of VRAM is the minimum, but 16GB is ideal. Just get the best card you can afford with at least 12GB or 16GB VRAM. *(Side note: if you do things other than gaming, Nvidia‚Äôs CUDA cores have been supported on most creative programs for years and this means most creative apps get a big boost to rendering and encoding thanks to the many years of updates he explains this in the video I linked really well.)*

- **Cooler**: For air cooling, grab the most expensive Noctua cooler (like the NH-D15) that fits your budget. If you prefer an AIO, the **ARCTIC Liquid Freezer** offers solid performance at a good price.

- **Motherboard**: Never skimp on the motherboard. A decent board in the $220‚Äì$350 range will cover your needs, but if you‚Äôre going with AMD, investing in a better board is a smart move. AMD tends to support boards longer, so you could upgrade the CPU in a few years without changing the board.

- **Storage**: An **NVMe SSD** is the only thing I‚Äôd consider these days since they‚Äôre so affordable. For storage size, 1TB drives are really cheap now. I can‚Äôt stress enough how important a DRAM buffer is‚Äîif a drive has one, it‚Äôll say so. I recommend brands like **Samsung, SK Hynix, or Crucial**‚Äîthey‚Äôre all reliable.

- **RAM**: Simple‚Äîmore is always better. I wouldn‚Äôt go below 32GB, but 48GB is ideal, especially for gaming and productivity.

- **Case**: Go for airflow, airflow, airflow! A mesh design is key. As long as it gets good reviews for airflow (check out Gamers Nexus or Hardware Canucks for that), you‚Äôre good. Personally, I‚Äôd recommend **Fractal Design** or **Lian Li**‚Äîboth offer great airflow options. Tho If I had to recomend one case [Corsair 5000D AIRFLOW](https://www.corsair.com/us/en/p/pc-cases/cc-9011210-ww/5000d-airflow-tempered-glass-mid-tower-atx-pc-case-black-cc-9011210-ww) this case it as strait forward as they get 

- **PSU**: This one‚Äôs straightforward, thanks to the [PSU Tier List](https://cultists.network/140/psu-tier-list/). Finding a good power supply is easy‚Äîjust get one in the **A Tier**. 1000W PSUs are getting more affordable, so wait for a sale and go with a trusted brand. **Seasonic** is my favorite‚Äîthey‚Äôre reliable and top quality.
